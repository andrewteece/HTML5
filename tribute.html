Ansel Adams

American Photographer and Enviromentalist

"A great photograph is one that fully expresses what one feels, in the deepest sense, about what is being photographed."

Ansel Adams

Ansel Adams: 1902-1982

  • 1902 - Born in San Francisco on February 20th, only child of Olive and Charles
  • 1906 - Young Ansel is injured during the San Francisco earthquake, breaking his nose and scarring him for life in the process.
  • 1914 - Ansel teaches himself to play piano
  • 1915 - He is removed from mainstream schooling, taught by parents, tutors or himself.
  • 1916 - On a family vacation to Yosemite National Park, California his parents bought him a Kodak Box Brownie camera.
  • 1919 - Joins the Sierra Club and became "keeper" of the Sierra's Club's Le Conte Memorial Lodge
  • 1921 - Meets future wife, Virgina, daughter of Harry Best, owner of "Best's Studio", a Yosemite concession.
  • 1922 - First published photographs and writing appear in the Sierra Club's "Bulletin"
  • 1927 - Takes first publicly accalimed photography "Monolith, The Face of Half Dome"

  • 1928 - Marries Virgina Best in January. Becomes offical photographer for the Sierra Club
  • 1930 - Ansel Adams publishes his first book after five trips to New Mexico, Tao Pueblo. Meets photographer Paul Strand in Taos, this helps fix Ansel on the course of photography rather than as a pianist.
  • 1932 - Forms f/64 photographic group with Edward Weston, Imogen Cunningham, Willard Van Dyke, Sonya Noskowiak, and Henry Swift.


  • 1935 - The first of the books dealing with the mastery of photographic technique, "Making a Photograph, is published.
  • 1936 - One-man show in New York with the aid of Alfred Stieglitz.
  • 1937 - Moves to Yosemite National Park. A fire in the darkroom destroys 5,000 negatives.
  • 1938 - Publishes "Sierra Nevada: The John Muir Trail.
  • 1940 - Teaches first Yosemite workshop, the U.S. Camera Photgraphic Forum, with Edward Weston.
  • 1941 - Develops "Zone System" of photography, a technique incorporating exposure and subsequent development of the negative
  • 1944 - Work from wartime photo essay on the plight of interned Japanese-Americans exhibited at the Museum of Modern Art (MOMA) un the title "Born Free and Equal".
  • 1946 - Helps establish the Department of Photography at the California School of Fine Art (later the San Francisco Art Institute)First Guggenheim Fellowship awarded.
  • 1947 - Leaves Yosemite National Park to setup studio in San Francisco ( a business could not legally be run within the confines of the park).
  • 1948 - Second Guggenheim Fellowship awarded. Results in publication of: Portfolio 2: "The National Parks and Monuments."
  • 1949 - Becomes consultant for Polaroid
  • 1953 - Collaborates with Dorothea Lange on a life magazine commission for a photo essay on the Mormons of Utah
  • 1960 - Third Guggenheim Fellowship, results in publication of: Portfolio 3: "Yosemite Valley"


  • 1962 - Moves to Carmel, California
  • 1965 - Named to President Johnson's environmental taks force
  • 1966 - Elected a "Fellow of the American Academy of Arts and Sciences"
  • 1975 - Helps found "Center for Creative Photography" at University of Arizona. Adams archives established there.
  • 1979 - "Yosemite and the Range of Light", published which was to sell over 200,000 copies
  • 1980 - "The Ansel Adams Conservation Award" established by the Wildreness Club. Adams is also awarded the "Presidential Medal of Freedom" from President Carter.
  • 1982 - At his 80th birthday celebration he is presented the Decoration of "Commander of the Order of the Arts and Letters", the highest cultural awared given by the French government to a foreigner.
  • 1984 - Ansel Adams passes away at the age of 82
  • 1985 - Sucessful legislation creates an "Ansel Adams Wilderness Area" of more than 100,000 acres between Yosemite National Park and the John Muir Wilderness Area.
  • 1986 - Offical naming of "Mount Ansel Adams", a 11,760 foot peak located at the head of the Lyell Fork of the Merced River on the southeast boundary of Yosemite National Park
